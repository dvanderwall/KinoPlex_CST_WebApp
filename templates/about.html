KinoPlex: A Structural Atlas of Human Phosphorylation

Decoding kinase specificity across the human proteome through AI-powered structural analysis

Overview

KinoPlex is a comprehensive structural atlas that predicts phosphorylation potential and kinase specificity for all 1.8 million serine, threonine, and tyrosine residues in the human proteome.

Phosphorylation is a critical protein modification that regulates nearly all cellular processes. However, among the 1.8 million potential phosphorylation sites in human proteins, only ~6% have been experimentally validated, and fewer than 5% of these have been mapped to specific kinases. KinoPlex bridges this gap by combining AlphaFold structural predictions with machine learning to systematically predict which sites can be phosphorylated and which kinases are likely responsible.

Our Approach

1

Structural Feature Extraction

We analyzed ~20,000 AlphaFold protein structures, extracting over 100 structural features for each of the 1.8 million STY residues. These features include solvent accessibility, disorder predictions, packing density, and local electrostatics.

2

Machine Learning Classification

Using positive-unlabeled transfer learning with XGBoost, we trained models to identify structurally phospho-competent sites. Our models achieve corrected AUCs of ~0.92 for S/T and 0.91 for Y residues.

3

Kinase Motif Scoring

We scored all sites against position-specific scoring matrices (PSSMs) for over 400 kinases, derived from systematic peptide library screening data, to predict kinase-substrate relationships.

4

Integrated Predictions

By intersecting structural phospho-competency with kinase motif scores, we identify ~250,000 high-confidence phosphorylation sites with both optimal structure and kinase recognition potential.

Key Scientific Insights

Structural Gating

~70% of STY residues are structurally prohibited from phosphorylation regardless of sequence context, explaining why only a small fraction of potential sites are actually phosphorylated.

Kinase Selection Strategies

Kinases employ divergent strategies: some achieve specificity through "structural scarcity" (targeting rare accessible motifs), while others gain broad influence through "structural promiscuity."

Motif-Structure Coupling

Linear motifs encode their own structural presentation - phosphorylation-prone motifs inherently favor disordered, accessible conformations.

Evolutionary Conservation

The structural constraints governing phosphorylation are evolutionarily conserved across species, suggesting fundamental biophysical principles.
